Telecom Customer Churn EDA - Graph Analysis

======================== analyze.ipynb ========================

1.  Churn Distribution

-   Around 26% of customers have churned, while 74% are retained.
-   The dataset is moderately imbalanced, so evaluation metrics like
    Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC should be considered along
    with accuracy.

2.  Tenure Distribution

-   Most customers have relatively short tenures.
-   The dataset contains many new customers and fewer long-term
    customers.

3.  MonthlyCharges Distribution

-   Monthly charges range from approximately 18 to 118.
-   Charges are spread across different service plans without major
    outliers.

4.  TotalCharges Distribution

-   Total charges are right-skewed.
-   Most customers have lower lifetime spending, while long-term
    customers accumulate much higher charges.

======================== day2.ipynb ========================

5.  Gender vs Churn

-   Male and female customers show nearly identical churn rates.
-   Gender has little influence on churn.

6.  SeniorCitizen vs Churn

-   Senior citizens have a higher churn rate.
-   Age group contributes moderately to churn prediction.

7.  Partner vs Churn

-   Customers without a partner churn more frequently.
-   Having a partner indicates greater customer stability.

8.  Dependents vs Churn

-   Customers without dependents are more likely to churn.
-   Customers with dependents tend to stay longer.

9.  Contract vs Churn

-   Month-to-month contracts have the highest churn.
-   Two-year contracts have the lowest churn.
-   Contract type is one of the strongest predictors of churn.

10. PaperlessBilling vs Churn

-   Customers using paperless billing churn more frequently.
-   This may be associated with month-to-month contracts.

11. PaymentMethod vs Churn

-   Electronic Check users have the highest churn.
-   Automatic payment methods are associated with better customer
    retention.

12. Tenure Groups vs Churn

-   Customers in their first year have the highest churn.
-   Churn decreases steadily as tenure increases.

13. TotalCharges Groups vs Churn

-   Customers with lower total charges churn more often.
-   Higher total charges indicate long-term, retained customers.

======================== day3.ipynb ========================

14. InternetService vs Churn

-   Fiber Optic customers have the highest churn.
-   DSL customers churn less, while customers without internet service
    churn the least.

15. PhoneService vs Churn

-   Phone service has very little effect on churn.
-   Churn rates are similar for both groups.

16. MultipleLines vs Churn

-   Customers with multiple lines show a slightly higher churn rate.
-   The difference is relatively small.

17. OnlineSecurity vs Churn

-   Customers without online security churn significantly more.
-   Online security improves customer retention.

18. OnlineBackup vs Churn

-   Customers without online backup churn more frequently.
-   Additional services improve retention.

19. DeviceProtection vs Churn

-   Customers without device protection are more likely to churn.
-   Protection services encourage customer loyalty.

20. TechSupport vs Churn

-   Customers without technical support have a much higher churn rate.
-   Tech support is one of the strongest retention-related services.

21. StreamingTV vs Churn

-   Streaming TV users show a slightly higher churn rate.
-   The effect is weaker than contract type or tenure.

22. StreamingMovies vs Churn

-   Streaming movie users also show a slightly higher churn rate.
-   This feature alone is not a strong predictor.

23. MonthlyCharges Boxplot

-   Churned customers generally pay higher monthly charges.
-   Higher monthly costs may contribute to churn.

24. TotalCharges Boxplot

-   Churned customers usually have lower total charges because they
    leave earlier.
-   Long-term customers accumulate much higher total charges.

25. Tenure Boxplot

-   Churned customers have much shorter tenures.
-   Retained customers stay significantly longer.

26. Correlation Heatmap

-   Most features have weak to moderate correlations.
-   Contract type, tenure, online security, and tech support show
    stronger relationships with churn.
-   No severe multicollinearity is observed.

======================== Overall EDA Summary ========================

-   Approximately 26% of customers have churned.
-   Month-to-month contracts experience the highest churn.
-   Short-tenure customers are most likely to churn.
-   Higher monthly charges are associated with increased churn.
-   Fiber Optic customers have the highest churn among internet service
    types.
-   Customers without Online Security, Tech Support, Online Backup, or
    Device Protection are more likely to churn.
-   Long-tenure customers, two-year contracts, and automatic payment
    methods are associated with higher retention.
-   Gender has little effect on churn, while SeniorCitizen has a
    moderate impact.
-   Contract type, tenure, monthly charges, and value-added services are
    the most important features for predicting churn.
